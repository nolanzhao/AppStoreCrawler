Description

Important notice: ING has decided to exit the retail banking market in the Philippines before end of 2022. Customers may continue to access their money via the ING app.


Important message from ING:


We have recently announced that ING has decided to leave the retail banking market in the Philippines. As a result, our retail banking services (deposits and loans) and the ING mobile banking app will be discontinued before the end of 2022. For now, customers may continue to access their accounts anytime and the other services via the ING app.


Continue to experience safe banking anytime, anywhere


• We're a universal bank supervised and regulated by the Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas (BSP). We're also a member of the Philippine Deposit Insurance Corporation (PDIC), which means deposits are insured up to PHP 500,000.


• Our app is built with the latest technology to ensure a high level of security for your data and money.


For more information, please visit our website: https://www.ing.com.ph/

ZEK-A , 25/12/2021

Simple, easy, fun

I wanted to open an account to celebrate myself being 18 by opening another bank account on my own. I researched all the bank accounts available out there, and ING lined up the most with what I needed today: no minimum balance, no transfer fees to other banks (via PESOnet, tho I wish Instapay will be added soon), no fuss application, and easy to use app + fun costumer service. I also love the free debit card, plus a free card holder (!) that can hold my other important cards. They even have promos that can use both Save and Pay even better!



I can also see them adding more features in the future: the aforementioned Instapay channel for transfer, at reasonable fees; a transfer channel to remittances + PayPal, cash in + channels in stuff like Seven-Eleven, groceries, department stores, and bills payments, an option to expand service to increase parity with ING in other countries, and the most important part: Adding ING to Apple Pay and Google Pay NFC payments for use here in the Philippines.


Two months into my account, and I still find wonders into how simple, easy, and fun banking with ING can be, and I can’t go wrong with them, with a storied track record both here, the Netherlands (their HQ), and around the world. I can’t wait for more from you guys. Thanks!
